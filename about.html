Introduction

De Moivre’s theorem is a formula that links complex numbers with trigonometry. It was developed by Abraham de Moivre, a French mathematician who made significant contributions to analytical geometry and theory of probability.Another important contribution of de Moivre was the Sterling’s approximation, a formula to give the approximate value of the factorial functions.
It is given by (cos θ + isin θ)n = cos nθ + isin nθ , for all integer n.
